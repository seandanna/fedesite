The story

From Buenos Aires,
with a six-string.

Hola. I'm Federico — Fede to the people who matter. I've been playing guitar for over twenty years, and the first time I picked one up I knew it was going to be for life. No exaggeration. Just the truth.

I work as both a musician and a teacher, and I'm a graduate of E.M.P.A. in Buenos Aires. Teaching isn't a side gig for me — it's the other half of the same passion. Watching a student go from "I can't" to ripping through their first solo is the kind of thing that keeps me plugged in.

Every student has their own way of learning.
My job is to figure out yours — and build the lessons around it.

Whether you're a total beginner who's never held a pick, or you've been strumming for years and want to finally understand what's happening under your fingers — we'll meet you where you are and push from there.

How we roll

The three
pillars.

  • 01

    Analyze every song

    We don't just play tunes — we take them apart. How does the melody sit over the chords? Why does that change hit so hard? Understanding the why is what separates copying from creating.

  • 02

    Train your ear

    Audio perception is the muscle most players never work. We build it every lesson — so you can hear a progression and already know what's happening before you even reach for the fretboard.

  • 03

    Write your own stuff

    Patterns on melodies. Patterns in chord progressions. Once you see them, you can move them around, twist them, and build songs that are yours. That's the real endgame.
